Rows

To add a row in your page, just put your content inside a <div> tag with a row class. Here is down below the best way to set it up.

  • <div class="row">
  •     <p>You nailed it!</p>
  • </div>

Spread

The spread class will space out the elements all along the row.

  • <div class="row spread">
  •     <div class="col col-25">.col</div>
  •     <div class="col col-25">.col</div>
  •     <div class="col col-25">.col</div>
  • </div>

Columns

To add a column in your page, just put your content inside a <div> tag with a col class. By default, a col will cover all the space available.

  • <div class="row">
  •     <div class="col">.col</div>
  •     <div class="col">.col</div>
  • </div>
.col
.col

Col-0~100

You can add col-0~100 class, which will define the col width in percentage.

  • <div class="row">
  •     <div class="col col-25">.col</div>
  •     <div class="col col-25">.col</div>
  •     <div class="col col-50">.col</div>
  • </div>

Offset-0~100

You can also add offset-0~100 class, which will offset the col position in percentage.

  • <div class="row">
  •     <div class="col col-50">.col</div>
  •     <div class="col col-25 offset-25">.col</div>
  • </div>

Responsive

There are 5 defined breakpoint in Nailed :

- xt : extra
- lg : large
- md : medium
- sm : small
- tn : tiny

You can use them to define the size of your col in percentage depending on the size of your screen. Try to change the size of your browser, and look how it works !

  • <div class="row">
  •     <div class="col col-50 xt-75 lg-50 md-30 sm-50 tn-75">.col</div>
  • </div>
